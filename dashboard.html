Between 2021 and 2023, inflation across major economies surged to levels unseen since the 1980s, driven by post-COVID supply chain disruptions and unprecedented fiscal stimulus. Central banks responded with the fastest interest rate hiking cycle in four decades — yet outcomes diverged sharply. Japan, facing deflationary pressures rather than inflation, held rates near zero throughout, exposing how the same global shock produced radically different policy responses.
